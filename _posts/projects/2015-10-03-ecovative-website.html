Ecovative is a biomaterials company from Green Island, New York. They grow environmentally-friendly products using mushrooms. Yes, mushrooms.

We were hired to redesign Ecovative’s website. It was important that the new site had solid content and clear navigation.

The Project

Ecovative hired us to redesign their old website. The old website had confusing content and navigation. It was also difficult for Ecovative to make quick changes to the site. They needed a company that would listen to their needs, design a professional looking website, and maintain the website after it was finished.

It was important that the new website had:

  • A clear navigation and information structure
  • A fresh design with better typography
  • An admin panel where they could manage the website

The Client

Ecovative was founded by Eben Bayer and Gavin McIntyre during their time at RPI. Ecovative and Delight met years ago when we interned for them in high school.

Their innovative mushroom material can be made into packaging insulation, acoustics, furniture, buoys, wedding dresses, lamp shades, and many other things. They also sell a “Grow-It-Yourself” material you can use to grow anything you can imagine.

Onboarding

Improving the onboarding process was a big focus of ours during the redesign. First, we ask users to sign up using LinkedIn, Facebook, or their email address. Second, they arrive on the Dashboard page where they have the option to continue filling out their profile. They also have the option to explore the site at this point and begin posting/applying to jobs.

Landing Page

Dashboard

Posting a Job

Once a business owner has signed on to the site, we want them to post a job. It was important that the Job Posting process was simple and painless. We ask the user a few quick questions, they click Post Job, and their job is posted. After this, they sit and wait for the applications to roll in.

Posting a Job

Finding a Job

For students, finding a job is just as easy as posting a job. Users can scroll through a list of jobs in their area and use Advanced Search to narrow down the list. Once they find a job they’re interested in, they just click “Apply”. Easy Peasy Lemon Squeezy.

Finding a Job

Map View

Students can also search for a job using the Map View. The map uses Google Maps API and places pins on jobs near the user’s location.
